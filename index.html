Geotechnical aspects of waterfront development

Horseshoe Casino Cincinnati
1000 Broadway

Communities such as Cincinnati are reclaiming their lost rivers and waterfronts to address environmental concerns, changing flood elevations, and to provide revitalized green space for city centers. As professionals in the geotechnical field, we are being asked to evaluate existing flood defenses and water retention structures and to provide sustainable design and construction services in areas with high water tables and rapid drawdown concerns.

The 45th Ohio River Valley Soil Seminar (ORVSS XLV) will address geotechnical aspects of waterfront development including geotechnical design, instrumentation, exploration, construction and case histories. ORVSS XLV will highlight the evolution of levee safety, pressure relief well assessment and design, scour protection, landslide repair along waterfronts, waterfront protection, riverfront development design and construction, rapid drawdown, and levee repair.

ORVSS XLV will provide Geotechnical Engineers, Geologists, Contractors, Material Suppliers, and other geotechnical practitioners an opportunity to share experiences on previous projects in the geotechnical aspects of waterfront development to continue the advancement of our professional field.

Speaker

Tammy L. Conforti, P.E.

Levee Safety Program Manager
441 G Street, NW
Room 3E62 Washington, DC 20314

Ms. Conforti has worked for the US Army Corps of Engineers for 23 years in a variety of areas related to flood risk management. In her current role as the headquarters Levee Safety Program Manager for USACE, she is responsible for overseeing implementation of the agency’s Levee Safety Program. This involves leading the development and issuance of program policies, serving as technical advisor on levee safety aspects and issues, prioritizing the agency’s program activities, and fostering partnerships by serving on related national committees and task forces. Ms. Conforti has a B.S. in civil engineering from Virginia Tech and is a registered professional engineer.

Ms. Conforti will present “The Evolution of Levee Safety within the U.S.” Topics discussed will include national challenges such as: state of the infrastructure, development patterns, expectations and behavior, and resource constraints and other considerations. The transformation from “levees” to “levee safety” will be discussed. Other topics include: implementing the USACE Levee Safety Program Risk Framework, USACE/ FEMA joint efforts, and USACE Levee related policy updates.
